Research interests

Genome-Wide Association Studies (GWAS)

Genome-Wide Association Studies (GWAS) represent a cornerstone in contemporary genetic research, offering a comprehensive approach to unraveling the genetic basis of complex traits and diseases. GWAS involves scanning the entire genome for variations in DNA sequences to identify associations between specific genetic markers and phenotypic traits. This approach has significantly advanced our understanding of the heritability of various conditions, providing critical insights into disease susceptibility and treatment responses. As a prospective Ph.D. student in bioinformatics, my research interest in GWAS lies in developing and applying innovative computational methods to efficiently analyze large-scale genomic data. I aim to contribute to refining the accuracy and interpretability of GWAS findings, ultimately enhancing our ability to decipher the intricate interplay between genetics and complex phenotypes.

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq)

Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) has emerged as a transformative technology in the field of genomics, allowing for unprecedented insights into the heterogeneity of gene expression at the individual cell level. This revolutionary technique enables the characterization of diverse cell populations within complex tissues, shedding light on cellular diversity, rare cell types, and dynamic changes in gene expression patterns. My research interest in single-cell RNA-seq analysis centers on the development and optimization of computational methodologies to extract meaningful information from high-dimensional single-cell data. I am particularly intrigued by the challenges posed by noisy data, sparsity, and the need for sophisticated algorithms to infer cell states, trajectories, and interactions. Through my Ph.D. studies, I aspire to contribute to the refinement of analytical tools, leveraging machine learning and statistical approaches, to unravel the intricacies of cellular heterogeneity and uncover novel biological insights that may have implications for understanding disease mechanisms and designing targeted therapeutic strategies.

AI/ML methods for biological predictions

AI and Machine Learning (ML) play a transformative role in decoding biological intricacies, particularly in genomics and drug discovery. In genomics, these methodologies excel at pinpointing disease-related genetic variations and unraveling complex gene expression patterns. This precision in analyzing high-throughput sequencing data enables the identification of subtle genetic markers associated with diseases, facilitating advancements in personalized medicine. Additionally, in drug discovery, AI/ML models streamline the identification of potential candidates by analyzing molecular structures and predicting interactions.

As a prospective Ph.D. student in bioinformatics, my research focus centers on advancing AI/ML techniques tailored for accurate and applicable biological predictions. The emphasis on interpretability in these models is crucial, fostering a deeper understanding of the biological mechanisms and facilitating the translation of computational insights into actionable outcomes in real-world biomedical applications. Through this research, my goal is to contribute to the development of robust, interpretable AI/ML tools, bridging the gap between computational advancements and their practical implications for healthcare outcomes and biomedical research.
